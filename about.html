Here's a little bit of who I am.

Why I Build Things

I love watching ideas come to life. That's really it. Mechatronics pulled me in because it sits at the intersection of everything I find interesting — electronics (I got hooked the first time I watched someone solder), mechanical design, and code. I've always wanted to build things, and robotics just made sense as the place where all of that comes together.

I mostly hate school, but I love the subjects that actually matter to me. The ones where I leave class and immediately want to go apply something, or simply subjects that are indespensable for daily life — that includes math, of course. That gap — between what's required and what fires me up — is probably why I end up doing a lot on my own. I built a program to calculate black hole evaporation rates and energy output because the physics genuinely fascinated me, not for some grade. I built an app that fetched and downloaded Spotify songs before I even knew tools like that already existed. That's kind of how my brain works.

Where I'm Actually From

I grew up in Mexico and moved to Northville a little over a year ago. I don't really try to talk about it, I don't hide it either, but I'd rather be defined by the change I make to my surroundings, not by the change the surroundings have in me.

The KIA STEM program at Liceo de Apodaca is probably the thing I'm most proud of because of what it meant to me and the people I did it with — my closest friends, the best teacher I had.

The Long Game

My actual goal in life has nothing to do with a corner office. I want to work hard enough, for long enough, to eventually buy a piece of land — grow things, plant trees, build a forest that outlives me and gets passed down to whoever comes after. Engineering is the path that gets me there. With some luck, it'll be a path I actually enjoy walking.
